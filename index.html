Revenue Leadership

We want to move at Revenue Lab speed.
The expertise is here. The infrastructure to make it travel automatically isn't — yet.

This team has the talent, the positioning, and the frameworks. The gap is infrastructure: right now, expertise doesn't travel automatically. The architecture fixes that: build the knowledge once, and every team inherits it automatically.

How the three layers work together
🗄️
Data layer — your existing stack
HubSpot, Google Drive, Asana, Slack, Zoom, G2, Guru — live inputs that feed context into every output without manual pulling.
🧠
Skills layer — your team's expertise
Marketing psychology, messaging frameworks, persona wrappers, sales methodology, PMM positioning — packaged once, available to all.
🚀
Application layer — teams ship faster
Teams say "launch a campaign" or "draft a co-sell one-pager" and automatically pull correct positioning, messaging, and brand.
📡
Org-level vs. team-level skills
PMM and brand skills apply automatically to everyone. Team-specific skills layer on top for their team only.

Decisions to make in this meeting
1
Does Revenue become its own Claude org?
Org-level plugins apply automatically to everyone. This is what makes PMM and brand skills the default for every output.
2
Can Colleen lead AI skills buildout across teams?
A dedicated person driving cross-team skills buildout accelerates everything. Without that, progress depends on each team lead carving out time independently.
3
Who is the operational lead for this rollout?
Someone needs to own day-to-day momentum, unblock teams, and run check-ins. Currently TBD.
4
Each team lead: what are your 2-3 highest-value skills?
The workflows your team does repeatedly, that are time-consuming, and that follow consistent rules — those are the first skills to build.
